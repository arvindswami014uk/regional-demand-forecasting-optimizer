Day 11 Executive Narrative — Green-Fast Fulfillment

Objective
Day 11 extends the capstone from forecasting and inventory policy into sustainable fulfillment decision support.
The project now recommends how the fulfillment network should respond to forecasted regional demand while balancing service, cost, and carbon intensity.

Confirmed carry-forward benchmarks
The forecasting foundation remains the Day 8 XGBoost model with 11.06% WMAPE, materially better than the 19.25% MA7 baseline.
The inventory foundation carries forward Day 9 policy results including \$157,292.60 in safety stock value, \$21,370.33 in annual storage cost, and \$780,850.78 in replenishment requirement.

What was built today
A synthetic carbon-emissions proxy for comparative planning analysis was introduced to estimate carbon-to-serve by lane.
The notebook compares four fulfillment strategies: Speed-first, Cost-first, Carbon-first, and Balanced.
Because richer raw network files were not confirmed in the repo, a data-anchored synthetic network was built from inventory policy and master data.

Decision framework
Speed-first prioritizes fastest delivery performance.
Cost-first prioritizes lower spend but can expose service risk if network flexibility is constrained.
Carbon-first prioritizes lower-carbon fulfillment paths, especially same-region allocation.
Balanced combines service, cost, and carbon into a practical operating recommendation.

Current recommended strategy: Carbon-first
This recommendation is based on a blended service-cost-carbon scorecard under the base scenario.
It should be treated as a decision-support policy, supported by planner overrides, scenario rules, and governance controls.

Strategic implication
The capstone now behaves like a sustainable network planning case study and an operations strategy product, not only a forecasting notebook.

Important disclosure
Estimated carbon outputs are synthetic proxies and must not be presented as measured emissions.
They are intended for comparative planning analysis only.

Next business upgrade
The next maturity step is to replace synthetic lane assumptions with measured distance, carrier performance, and actual carbon factors.
