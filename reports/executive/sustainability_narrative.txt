Sustainability Narrative — Carbon-Aware Allocation and Green-Fast Fulfillment

This project uses a synthetic carbon-emissions proxy for comparative planning analysis.
Because confirmed shipment distance and measured emissions data were not available in the working dataset, transparent lane-based factors were used instead of claiming precise carbon accounting.

The sustainability logic is operational.
Same-region fulfillment is treated as lower-carbon and typically faster than unnecessary cross-region movement.
Cross-region lanes are assigned progressively higher carbon factors as lead time increases.

This enables a direct comparison between cost-to-serve and carbon-to-serve.
It also makes same-region fulfillment a measurable operating metric rather than a qualitative aspiration.

The business objective is not carbon minimization at any cost.
The objective is green-fast fulfillment: lowering avoidable carbon intensity while preserving delivery promise performance and controlling fulfillment economics.

This makes sustainability part of planning and network design.
It is embedded in warehouse choice, rebalance logic, planner overrides, and scenario analysis.

As a result, the capstone now frames sustainability as a decision variable in fulfillment planning, not just a reporting add-on.
